The 2025 Composite Classification Thresholds and Reverse Graduation DynamicsThe formalization of the revised MSME classification thresholds was enacted through Gazette Notification S.O. 1364(E) dated March 21, 2025, issued by the Ministry of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises. This update is intended to help businesses scale up, adopt modern technologies, and access credit without prematurely losing the regulatory protections and financial subsidies associated with MSME status.The table below compares the 2020 composite framework with the revised 2025 criteria:Evolution of Composite MSME Classification ThresholdsEnterprise CategoryPrevious Investment Limits (2020 Framework)Revised Investment Limits (Effective April 1, 2025)Previous Turnover Limits (2020 Framework)Revised Turnover Limits (Effective April 1, 2025)Micro$\le$ ₹1 Crore$\le$ ₹2.5 Crore$\le$ ₹5 Crore$\le$ ₹10 CroreSmall$\le$ ₹10 Crore$\le$ ₹25 Crore$\le$ ₹50 Crore$\le$ ₹100 CroreMedium$\le$ ₹50 Crore$\le$ ₹125 Crore$\le$ ₹250 Crore$\le$ ₹500 CroreUnder the composite classification logic, the transition of an enterprise between categories is governed by specific rules:An enterprise graduates to the next higher category if it exceeds either the investment or the turnover limit designated for its current class.An enterprise can only be reclassified into a lower category (reverse graduation) if both its investment and turnover fall below the thresholds of its current class.This composite approach prevents minor, temporary spikes in annual sales from immediately stripping an enterprise of its micro or small status. Furthermore, under Clause 8(6) of the principal MSMED circular, any change in classification due to reclassification or actual changes in investment/turnover does not immediately impact the enterprise's regulatory status. The enterprise continues in its existing category until the closure of the current financial year, and the effects of reclassification apply only from April 1 of the subsequent financial year. For reverse graduation under the expanded 2025 limits, eligible enterprises retained their existing status until March 31, 2025, with the revised, more favorable classification taking effect on April 1, 2025.
