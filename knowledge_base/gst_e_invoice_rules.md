The Indian GST e-Invoicing Framework: Rules and Turnover MandatesThe Indian Goods and Services Tax (GST) e-invoicing framework, introduced on the recommendation of the GST Council, is a key component of the country's tax administration. Governed by Rule 48 of the CGST Rules, 2017, the system requires designated taxpayers to report specified B2B transactions to an authorized IRP.As established in Rule 48(4), an invoice is only valid under GST law if it carries a verified IRN and a signed QR code. Under Rule 48(5), any invoice issued without this validation is legally void, meaning the buyer cannot claim Input Tax Credit (ITC) and the supplier may face penalties.The e-invoicing mandate has been rolled out in a phased manner, progressively lowering the turnover threshold to bring mid-market and small businesses into the compliance loop :Implementation PhaseEffective DateTurnover Threshold (PAN-Level Aggregate Annual Turnover - AATO)Phase IOctober 1, 2020Exceeding ₹500 Crore in any preceding FY from 2017-18 onwards.Phase IIJanuary 1, 2021Exceeding ₹100 Crore in any preceding FY from 2017-18 onwards.Phase IIIApril 1, 2021Exceeding ₹50 Crore in any preceding FY from 2017-18 onwards.Phase IVApril 1, 2022Exceeding ₹20 Crore in any preceding FY from 2017-18 onwards.Phase VOctober 1, 2022Exceeding ₹10 Crore in any preceding FY from 2017-18 onwards.Phase VIAugust 1, 2023Exceeding ₹5 Crore in any preceding FY from 2017-18 onwards.The threshold is calculated using the aggregate annual turnover under a single Permanent Account Number (PAN) across all GST registrations in India, rather than looking at individual branches or GSTINs. If an enterprise crosses the ₹5 crore threshold in any financial year from 2017-18 onwards, e-invoicing remains permanently mandatory, even if the business’s turnover later drops below the limit. If a business crosses the threshold mid-year, e-invoicing becomes mandatory from the first day of the following financial year.However, the e-invoicing rules do not apply uniformly to all businesses or transactions. The system categorizes applicability based on the entity type and the nature of the transaction :Mandated Documents and Transactions: The system covers all B2B transactions, supplies to government departments (B2G), exports (EXP), transactions with Special Economic Zone (SEZ) Developers, deemed exports, and associated credit and debit notes.Exempted Entities: Banks, financial institutions, insurance companies, Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs), Goods Transport Agencies (GTAs), passenger transport providers, multiplexes, OIDAR service providers, and SEZ Units are exempted from the e-invoicing mandate under CBIC notifications. (While SEZ Units are exempt, SEZ Developers must generate e-invoices if they cross the turnover threshold).Excluded Documents and Transactions: Delivery challans, Bills of Supply, import bills, high sea sales, sales in bonded warehouses, and direct business-to-consumer (B2C) sales are excluded from the e-invoicing mandate.A key requirement in this compliance framework is the 30-day reporting window, which applies to businesses with an aggregate annual turnover of ₹10 crore and above. Under guidelines issued by the GSTN, these taxpayers must report their invoices to the IRP within 30 days of issuance. For example, if an invoice is issued on October 1, 2025, it must be reported to the portal by October 30, 2025. If the 30-day reporting window is missed, the portal will reject the invoice, rendering it invalid for tax purposes and preventing the buyer from claiming Input Tax Credit.
