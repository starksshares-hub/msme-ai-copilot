Cash flow means the movement of money in and out of a business.

Cash inflow means money coming into the business. Examples include:

* Customer payments
* Sales revenue
* Loan received
* Advance payment from customers

Cash outflow means money going out of the business. Examples include:

* Vendor payments
* Rent
* Salaries
* Electricity bills
* Inventory purchases
* Loan repayment
* GST or tax payments

Cash flow is different from profit. A business can be profitable but still have cash flow problems if customers pay late.

Common cash flow problems faced by MSMEs:

* Late customer payments
* High expenses before sales money is received
* Poor tracking of daily cash
* Unexpected vendor payments
* Too much money stuck in inventory
* No clear monthly cash forecast

An AI copilot can help by:

* Showing expected cash balance
* Warning about upcoming cash shortages
* Tracking unpaid invoices
* Summarizing monthly inflow and outflow
* Explaining why cash is low
* Suggesting actions like collecting overdue payments or reducing unnecessary expenses

Example questions:

* What is my expected cash balance this month?
* Why is my cash flow negative?
* Which payments are due soon?
* How much cash came in this week?
* How much money went out this month?
