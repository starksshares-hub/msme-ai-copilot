## Expense Category Notes

### Rent

The business paid ₹25,000 as monthly factory rent. Rent is a fixed operating expense because it does not directly change with sales volume.

### Salary and Wages

Salary and wages were ₹1,20,000 for April 2026. This includes monthly worker wages and basic staff payments. Salary is usually one of the largest regular expenses for an MSME.

### Electricity

Electricity cost was ₹18,500. Since this is a manufacturing unit, electricity is important for operating machines. A sudden rise in electricity expense may indicate higher production, machine inefficiency, or billing issues.

### Raw Materials

Raw material expense was ₹2,40,000, which is the highest expense category in this report. This includes paper rolls, adhesive, coating material, packing tape, and labels. Raw material cost directly affects gross profit.

### Transport and Delivery

Transport and delivery expense was ₹32,000. This includes local delivery and intercity logistics. High transport cost may reduce profit if it is not charged separately to customers.

### Marketing

Marketing expense was ₹12,000 for local digital advertisements. This is an operating expense used to generate customer leads and sales.

### Software and Subscriptions

Software expense was ₹5,500. This includes accounting software and cloud backup subscriptions. These expenses support business operations but should be reviewed regularly to avoid unused subscriptions.

### Repairs and Maintenance

Repair expense was ₹9,000 for machine servicing. Maintenance expenses help avoid production delays, but frequent repairs may indicate old or inefficient machinery.

### Office Supplies

Office supplies cost ₹4,200. This includes stationery, files, paper, and printing material.

### Miscellaneous Expenses

Miscellaneous expenses were ₹3,800. These are small daily business expenses. Miscellaneous costs should be monitored because too many untracked small expenses can reduce cash flow.

---

## Key Observations

1. Total expenses for April 2026 were ₹4,70,000.
2. Raw materials were the largest expense, making up more than half of total expenses.
3. Salary and wages were the second-largest expense.
4. Fixed expenses such as rent and salaries must be paid even if sales are low.
5. Variable expenses such as raw materials, transport, and electricity may increase when production increases.
6. Cash payments were made for some raw materials, office supplies, and miscellaneous expenses. These should be properly recorded with bills or vouchers.
7. The business should compare these expenses with monthly revenue to calculate net profit.
8. If expenses are rising faster than sales, the business may face cash flow pressure.
