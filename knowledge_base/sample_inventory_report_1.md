## Meaning of Inventory Report

An inventory report shows the stock available in a business.

It helps the business understand:

* Which items are available.
* Which items are low in stock.
* Which items are overstocked.
* Which items are fast-moving.
* Which items are slow-moving.
* Which items are dead stock.
* How much money is blocked in inventory.

Inventory is important because stock directly affects sales, cash flow, and working capital.

**Cost of Goods Sold = Opening Inventory + Purchases - Closing Inventory**

## Inventory Turnover

Inventory turnover shows how quickly inventory is sold and replaced.

A higher inventory turnover usually means stock is moving quickly.

A lower inventory turnover may mean slow-moving or overstocked inventory.

### Inventory Turnover Formula

**Inventory Turnover = Cost of Goods Sold ÷ Average Inventory**

**Average Inventory = Opening Inventory + Closing Inventory ÷ 2**

Correct formula:

**Average Inventory = (Opening Inventory + Closing Inventory) ÷ 2**

For April 2024:

**Average Inventory = (₹2,80,000 + ₹2,35,000) ÷ 2**

**Average Inventory = ₹2,57,500**

**Inventory Turnover = ₹2,20,000 ÷ ₹2,57,500**

**Inventory Turnover = 0.85 times**

This means inventory turned over 0.85 times during the month.

---

## Inventory Days

Inventory days shows how many days stock stays in the business before being sold.

### Inventory Days Formula

**Inventory Days = Average Inventory ÷ Cost of Goods Sold × Number of Days**

For April 2024:

**Inventory Days = ₹2,57,500 ÷ ₹2,20,000 × 30**

**Inventory Days = 35.11 days**

This means inventory stays in the business for around 35 days before being sold.

## Inventory Risk Analysis

The business has three major inventory risks:

### 1. Stockout Risk

A4 Copier Paper Ream, Whiteboard Marker Pack, and Premium Notebook are low in stock.

These are important products because customers buy them frequently.

If the business does not reorder them, it may lose sales.

### 2. Overstock Risk

Office Chair Cushion has high closing stock compared to monthly sales.

Too much stock blocks cash and increases storage cost.

### 3. Slow-Moving Stock Risk

Desk Organizer and Laptop Stand are selling slowly.

If these items remain unsold for many months, they may become dead stock.

---

## Inventory and Cash Flow

Inventory affects cash flow because money is spent before goods are sold.

If a business buys too much stock, cash gets blocked.

Example:

If ₹66,560 is blocked in slow-moving and overstocked inventory, the business may have less cash available for:

* Supplier payments
* Rent
* Salaries
* GST payments
* Marketing
* Emergency expenses

Good inventory management helps improve cash flow.

---

## Inventory and Working Capital

Inventory is part of current assets.

But inventory is not as liquid as cash.

The business must sell inventory first before it becomes cash.

If inventory does not sell quickly, working capital gets stuck.

Example:

A product may appear as an asset in the balance sheet, but if no customer buys it, it cannot help pay urgent bills.

---

## Inventory and Profit and Loss

Inventory affects cost of goods sold and gross profit.

If closing inventory is recorded incorrectly, profit may also be incorrect.

Example:

If closing inventory is overstated, cost of goods sold may look lower and profit may look higher.

If closing inventory is understated, cost of goods sold may look higher and profit may look lower.

Correct inventory records are necessary for accurate profit calculation.

---

## Warning Signs

The following signs show inventory problems:

* Fast-moving items are going out of stock.
* Slow-moving items are increasing.
* Dead stock is not being reviewed.
* Stock value is high but sales are low.
* Business purchases inventory without checking demand.
* Products are bought because of supplier discounts but do not sell.
* Closing stock is not counted properly.
* Inventory records do not match actual stock.
* Too much cash is blocked in unsold goods.
* Reorder levels are not defined.
* Theft, damage, expiry, or wastage is not tracked.

---

## Recommended Actions

The business should:

1. Reorder A4 Copier Paper Ream, Whiteboard Marker Pack, and Premium Notebook.
2. Avoid purchasing more Office Chair Cushions until existing stock reduces.
3. Offer discounts or bundles for Desk Organizer and Laptop Stand.
4. Review slow-moving items every month.
5. Set reorder levels for all important products.
6. Compare physical stock with system stock regularly.
7. Avoid buying excess inventory only because of supplier discounts.
8. Focus purchasing on fast-moving items.
9. Track inventory value, not only quantity.
10. Check whether inventory is blocking too much working capital.

## Simple Explanation for MSME Owner

Inventory is the stock your business has.

If you have too little stock, you may lose sales.

If you have too much stock, your money gets blocked.

In this report, some products like A4 Copier Paper Ream and Premium Notebook are selling fast and need reordering.

But products like Laptop Stand, Desk Organizer, and Office Chair Cushion are not moving quickly.

The business should buy more fast-moving items and reduce slow-moving stock.

## Final Conclusion

Sunrise Office Supplies has useful inventory records for April 2024, but the business must manage stock carefully.

Fast-moving products need timely reordering to avoid lost sales.

Slow-moving and overstocked products are blocking ₹66,560 of cash.

The business should review inventory every month, reduce unnecessary purchases, sell slow-moving products through discounts or bundles, and focus on products with strong demand.
