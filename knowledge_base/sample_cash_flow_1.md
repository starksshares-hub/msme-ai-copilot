# Sample Cash Flow Statement 1

## Business Profile

**Business Name:** Sunrise Office Supplies
**Business Type:** MSME trading business
**Industry:** Office stationery and supplies
**Period:** April 2024
**Currency:** INR

---

## Cash Flow Summary

A cash flow statement shows how cash enters and leaves a business during a period.

It is different from profit and loss because profit may include credit sales, unpaid bills, depreciation, and other non-cash items.

Cash flow focuses only on actual cash movement.

---

## Opening Cash Balance

Opening cash balance is the amount of cash available at the beginning of the period.

For April 2024, Sunrise Office Supplies had:

**Opening Cash Balance:** ₹85,000

---

## Cash Inflows

Cash inflows are sources from where money came into the business.

| Source of Cash Inflow                       |    Amount |
| ------------------------------------------- | --------: |
| Cash sales                                  | ₹1,20,000 |
| Customer payments received for old invoices | ₹2,40,000 |
| Short-term loan received                    | ₹1,00,000 |
| Owner capital introduced                    |   ₹50,000 |
| Interest income                             |    ₹2,000 |

**Total Cash Inflows:** ₹5,12,000

---

## Cash Outflows

Cash outflows are payments made by the business.

| Source of Cash Outflow          |    Amount |
| ------------------------------- | --------: |
| Supplier payments               | ₹2,10,000 |
| Employee salaries               |   ₹75,000 |
| Shop rent                       |   ₹35,000 |
| Electricity and utilities       |   ₹12,000 |
| Transport and delivery expenses |   ₹18,000 |
| Loan repayment                  |   ₹40,000 |
| GST payment                     |   ₹28,000 |
| Office maintenance              |    ₹9,000 |
| Miscellaneous expenses          |    ₹6,000 |

**Total Cash Outflows:** ₹4,33,000

---

## Net Cash Flow

Net cash flow is calculated as:

**Net Cash Flow = Total Cash Inflows - Total Cash Outflows**

For April 2024:

**Net Cash Flow = ₹5,12,000 - ₹4,33,000**

**Net Cash Flow = ₹79,000**

The business generated positive cash flow of ₹79,000 during the month.

---

## Closing Cash Balance

Closing cash balance is calculated as:

**Closing Cash Balance = Opening Cash Balance + Net Cash Flow**

For April 2024:

**Closing Cash Balance = ₹85,000 + ₹79,000**

**Closing Cash Balance = ₹1,64,000**

At the end of April 2024, Sunrise Office Supplies had ₹1,64,000 cash available.

---

## Cash Flow Statement Format

| Particulars               |    Amount |
| ------------------------- | --------: |
| Opening Cash Balance      |   ₹85,000 |
| Add: Total Cash Inflows   | ₹5,12,000 |
| Less: Total Cash Outflows | ₹4,33,000 |
| Net Cash Flow             |   ₹79,000 |
| Closing Cash Balance      | ₹1,64,000 |

---

## Cash Flow Interpretation

The business has a positive cash flow for the month.

This means more cash came into the business than went out.

A positive cash flow is usually a good sign because the business has enough money to pay suppliers, employees, rent, taxes, and other expenses.

However, part of the cash inflow came from a short-term loan of ₹1,00,000 and owner capital of ₹50,000.

This means the business did not generate all cash from operations.

Without the loan and owner capital, the cash inflow would have been:

**Operating Cash Inflow = ₹5,12,000 - ₹1,00,000 - ₹50,000**

**Operating Cash Inflow = ₹3,62,000**

Since cash outflow was ₹4,33,000, the business would have faced a cash shortage without external funding.

This shows that the business needs to improve collection from customers and control expenses.

---

## Key Observations

1. The closing cash balance increased from ₹85,000 to ₹1,64,000.
2. The business had a positive net cash flow of ₹79,000.
3. Customer payments received were the biggest source of cash inflow.
4. Supplier payments were the biggest cash outflow.
5. The business depended on a short-term loan and owner capital to maintain cash flow.
6. Without external funding, the business would have had negative cash flow.
7. GST payment, salaries, rent, and supplier payments created major cash pressure.

---

## Warning Signs

The following cash flow issues may create risk:

* Heavy dependency on customer collections.
* Large supplier payments compared to cash sales.
* Short-term loan used to support daily operations.
* GST payment causing cash pressure.
* Owner capital introduced to cover business needs.
* Business may be profitable but still face cash shortage if customers delay payment.

## RAG Answer Examples

### Question: What was the closing cash balance?

The closing cash balance was ₹1,64,000.

It was calculated by adding the net cash flow of ₹79,000 to the opening cash balance of ₹85,000.

---

### Question: Was the business cash flow positive or negative?

The business had positive cash flow.

Total cash inflows were ₹5,12,000 and total cash outflows were ₹4,33,000, resulting in positive net cash flow of ₹79,000.

---

### Question: Why is the business still risky even though cash flow is positive?

The business is still risky because ₹1,50,000 of the cash inflow came from external funding.

This includes ₹1,00,000 from a short-term loan and ₹50,000 from owner capital.

Without this external funding, the business would have faced a cash shortage.

---

### Question: What was the biggest cash outflow?

The biggest cash outflow was supplier payments of ₹2,10,000.

This means supplier payments created the highest cash pressure during the month.

---

### Question: What should the business do to improve cash flow?

The business should improve customer collections, reduce unnecessary expenses, negotiate better supplier payment terms, avoid depending too much on short-term loans, and maintain a cash reserve for rent, salaries, taxes, and emergency expenses.

---

## Simple Explanation

Cash flow tells whether the business actually has money available.

A business can show profit but still struggle if customers do not pay on time.

In this example, Sunrise Office Supplies had positive cash flow, but the positive result depended heavily on a short-term loan and owner capital.

This means the business should focus on collecting payments faster and reducing dependency on borrowed money.

---

## Important Formulae

**Net Cash Flow = Total Cash Inflows - Total Cash Outflows**

**Closing Cash Balance = Opening Cash Balance + Net Cash Flow**

**Operating Cash Inflow = Total Cash Inflow - Loan Received - Owner Capital Introduced**

---

## Final Conclusion

Sunrise Office Supplies ended April 2024 with a closing cash balance of ₹1,64,000.

The business generated positive net cash flow of ₹79,000.

However, the business relied on ₹1,50,000 of external funding through a short-term loan and owner capital.

The business should improve collections, reduce unnecessary expenses, and manage supplier payments carefully to avoid future cash shortages.
