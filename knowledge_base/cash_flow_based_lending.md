Empowering Cash-Flow-Based Lending ModelsThe lack of systematic financial tracking has historically contributed to a massive credit gap in the MSME sector, estimated at ₹20 lakh crore to ₹30 lakh crore. Traditional institutional lending relies on asset-backed credit, which requires borrowers to provide tangible collateral (such as real estate or machinery) to secure a loan.However, many micro and small enterprises are asset-light and do not possess high-value properties to pledge. Additionally, banks have struggled to assess credit risk due to a lack of formal credit histories and audited financial records among small businesses.To address this issue, the lending landscape is transitioning toward Cash-Flow-Based Lending (CFBL) models, supported by the Reserve Bank of India's (RBI) Account Aggregator framework. Under CFBL, financial institutions assess creditworthiness based on real-time cash inflows rather than tangible collateral.Lending algorithms analyze alternative digital data points to evaluate risk, including:GST Returns: Confirming sales volumes, operational consistency, and client diversity.Point-of-Sale (POS) and UPI Logs: Providing real-time evidence of daily business receipts.Digital Bank Statements: Tracking cash flow velocity, average monthly balances, and transaction history through secure Account Aggregator channels.By utilizing verified transaction data, lenders can offer customized, collateral-free credit products, such as revolving credit lines and small "sachet" loans with flexible repayment terms that align with the borrower's seasonal cash inflows. For the MSME, maintaining meticulous digital financial records is no longer just a compliance task; it is a strategic requirement for accessing affordable formal credit.
