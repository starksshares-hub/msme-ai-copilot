Operational Workflows and Tax Ledger IntegrationGSTR-1 Auto-Population and Input Tax Credit FlowA primary administrative benefit of the e-invoicing framework is the automated, electronic synchronization of transaction data across the national tax infrastructure. This integration connects the IRP directly with the central GST portal and the e-Way Bill generation systems, significantly reducing manual data entry and minimizing reconciliation errors.Once an invoice is validated and receives its IRN, the IRP transmits the structured transaction data to the GST system. This data is used to auto-populate the supplier’s outward supply return (Form GSTR-1).The transmission and auto-population workflow operates on a strict timeline, with details visible in GSTR-1 within two days of generation ($T+2$ basis).The structured e-invoice details are mapped to specific tables in the supplier’s GSTR-1 :B2B Table (4A): Captures standard taxable outward supplies made to registered buyers (excluding reverse charge transactions).EXP Table (6A): Captures export transactions, mapping the export category (such as "With Payment of Tax" or "Without Payment of Tax under LUT") and shipping details.CDNR Table (9B - Registered): Auto-populates credit or debit notes issued to registered buyers.CDNUR Table (9B - Unregistered): Auto-populates credit or debit notes associated with unregistered export supplies.To ensure clear audit trails, auto-populated records in GSTR-1 are flagged with their source ("e-invoice"), along with the corresponding IRN and the exact date of generation.If a taxpayer edits any of this auto-populated data while filing GSTR-1, the system flags the modified record. This modification breaks the direct link to the IRP, and the edited transaction is treated as a manual entry, which may trigger automated compliance scrutiny notices.For buyers, this real-time integration simplifies the reconciliation of Input Tax Credit (ITC). Verified e-invoice details flow directly into the buyer's GSTR-2B, which serves as a static, auto-drafted ITC ledger. This transparency ensures that buyers only claim genuine, validated ITCs, helping them avoid penalties and preventing the use of fake or duplicate invoices.
